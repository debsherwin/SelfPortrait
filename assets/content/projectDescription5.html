Problem

Educational publisher Zoobooks wanted to reach a new market—parents of children 0-3—in a cost-effective way.

Goals

  • Extend the Zoobooks magazine product line with age-appropriate content
  • Include interactive experiences for parents that go ‘beyond the page’
  • Create evergreen content that can be republished as children age out of the subscription

Process

I reviewed a wide range of books and magazines for very young children to understand what made for a visually engaging experience. I also researched key concepts in early childhood development which we could feature: high contrast, shape, color, motion, interpersonal relationships, and facial expressions.

This translated into a serializable magazine concept with a consistent structure: ‘meeting’ the animal, exploring the animal’s physical attributes and actions, and ending with a rhyme and a game. We offered parents a tip guide and prompted them to point, open flaps, make funny noises and offer snuggles at appropriate moments.

We used ‘elephants’ to demonstrate the concept, and I audited Zoobooks existing photography library to create an ‘editorial calendar’ of animals with the most assets available.

Outcome

The concept was approved and we were engaged to fully produce the first six issues, available at zoobooks.com.
